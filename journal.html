Dealing With Anxiety

Published: February 2023

With the news about big Tech companies laying off here and there, I can't help but feel anxious. Am I still on the right track in pursuing my career goals? What if the company I work for also starts laying off soon? Will I be skilled enough to compete and stand out in the job market? All these questions suddenly keep coming into my head. I'm glad I heard the church's Sunday message this morningâ€”such a great reminder of how important it is to walk by faith. I recalled God's work in my life, how he remained faithful all this time, and I'm suddenly at peace.

The pastor shared an incredible story about how a songwriter had to write 100 a year for two years before the publisher chose ONE song. I remembered my journey when I got back into the workforce after giving birth. Remote work was not a norm then, and getting my first gig was not easy. I had to send five applications a day for over a month. That's over 100 intentional applications sent until ONE employer took a chance on me. I knew God led me to the right door. I am genuinely grateful for my time at Lifestyle Barn. My experience there taught me the foundational skills to thrive in remote work and the tech industry. It was my entryway to the world of Saas products and has led me to Zapier.

This story isn't my "How-I-got-my-dream-job" success story but rather a story of how victorious I am with God and how he has carried me through. No one knows what the future holds. You may be at the peak of your career or business now, but all these things can change instantly. Your job, your business, or whatever your source of security is can be taken away from you, but we can rest in the fact that we have an unchanging God. We can always trust that he will take us wherever we need to be.

We all have life battles, and whatever you may be going through right now, I encourage you to look back at God's track record and how HE has sustained you. You may also be one of those who ask, what if there's none? You might say, I've been suffering all my life, yet where is God? or say I never felt his presence. I encourage you to seek him. These challenging times may be the time for you to seek him and know who he is. It will be worth your time. Start by listening to his word.
